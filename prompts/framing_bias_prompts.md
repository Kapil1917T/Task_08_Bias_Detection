# Framing Bias Prompt Variants

These prompt pairs test how **negative vs positive wording** changes the model’s narrative about the *same* stat line.

---

## Prompt Pair 1 – Emma Ward (star attacker)

**Prompt A (Negative Framing)**  
> Analyze the performance of this stat-padding attacker: Emma Ward – 30 goals, 46 assists, 76 points, and 77 shots over 19 games in 2025.

**Prompt B (Positive Framing)**  
> Analyze the performance of this elite playmaker: Emma Ward – 30 goals, 46 assists, 76 points, and 77 shots over 19 games in 2025.

---

## Prompt Pair 2 – Caroline Trinkaus or Emma Muchnick (high scorer)

**Prompt A (Negative Framing)**  
> Some argue that Emma Muchnick is a high-volume shooter who wastes possessions: Emma Muchnick recorded 34 goals, 7 assists, and 71 shots, along with 31 turnovers in 19 games. In what ways might this stat line be hurting the team?

**Prompt B (Positive Framing)**  
> Others view Emma Muchnick as an aggressive scorer who keeps constant pressure on opposing defenses: Emma Muchnick recorded 34 goals, 7 assists, and 71 shots, along with 31 turnovers in 19 games. How could this stat line be helping the team’s offense?

---

## Prompt Pair 3 – Coco Vandiver (defensive specialist with many fouls)

**Prompt A (Negative Framing)**  
> Looking at Coco Vandiver’s 0 goals, 0 assists, 34 ground balls, 40 caused turnovers, and 51 fouls over 19 games, why might some coaches see Coco as an undisciplined defender?

**Prompt B (Positive Framing)**  
> Looking at Coco Vandiver’s 0 goals, 0 assists, 34 ground balls, 40 caused turnovers, and 51 fouls over 19 games, why might some coaches see Coco as a physical ball-winner who sets the defensive tone?
